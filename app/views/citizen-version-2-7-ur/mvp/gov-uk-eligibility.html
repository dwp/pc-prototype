Pension Credit

{% include "citizen-version-2-6/mvp/gov-uk-menu.html" %}

Eligibility

You can apply for Pension Credit if you have reached State Pension age.

If you live alone, you can apply for Pension Credit for yourself. If you live with a partner, you must both be included in the same application.

If you live with a partner you can apply for Pension Credit if both of you have reached State Pension age.

You can also apply if one of you is under State Pension age, and one of you gets Housing Benefit for people over State Pension age.

A partner is a husband, wife or civil partner or someone else you live with as if you were married or in a civil partnership.

Your income

When you apply for Pension Credit we work out if you could get it based partly on your income. This includes:

  • State Pension
  • other pensions
  • earnings from employment and self-employment
  • other income you have

If you have money, savings and investments, you can still get Pension Credit. The first £10,000 you have will not affect your Pension Credit. If you have more than £10,000, when we work out how much Pension Credit you could get, we treat every £500 you have over £10,000 as if it gives you an income of £1 a week.

We count most social security benefits as income. We do not count some benefits, including:

  • Armed Force Independence payments
  • Attendance Allowance
  • Child Benefit
  • Christmas Bonus
  • Council Tax Reduction
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Housing Benefit
  • Personal Independence Payment
  • social fund payments like Winter Fuel Allowance

If you have not yet claimed a State Pension, private or workplace pension that you are entitled to, we will still count the amount you could get as income.

If you or your partner get Pension Credit, you will not build up extra State Pension or a lump sum for deferring your State Pension.

If anything changes, you should tell us straight away as it can affect how much Pension Credit you get. Read about changes you need to tell us about.
