Pension Credit

How to apply

You can apply online, by phone or by post.

When to apply

The earliest you can start your application is 4 months before you reach State Pension age.

If you apply after you have reached State Pension age, you can backdate your application by up to 3 months.

Information you will need

You will need:

  • your National Insurance number and your partner's, if you have one
  • information about any income, savings and investments you (and your partner, if you have one) have
  • your bank account details, if you are applying by phone or by post

If you backdate your claim, we will ask for a total balance of the money, savings and investments you have today and on the date you want to backdate your claim to. This is usually either 3 months ago or the date you reached your State Pension age.

Apply online

You can use the online service if:

  • you have already claimed your State Pension
  • there are no children or young people included in your claim

Apply by phone

A friend or family member can call for you if you cannot use the phone.

Apply by post

To apply by post, print out and fill in the Pension Credit claim form or call the claim line to ask for a form.

Send the claim form to the Pension Service, or ask someone to do it for you.

Contact a voluntary organisation such as Citizens Advice or Age UK if you need help with the form.

If you disagree with a decision

You can challenge a decision about your claim. This is called asking for mandatory reconsideration.
