Pension Credit

{% include "citizen-version-2-6/mvp/gov-uk-menu.html" %}

What you could get

The basic amount of Pension credit tops up your weekly income to £173.75 for single people or £265.20 for couples.

You could get more on top of the basic amount if you have other responsibilities and costs.

If you have a severe disability

You could get an extra payment of £66.95 a week if you get:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • the middle or highest rate care component of Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • Armed Forces Independence Payment (AFIP)

If you care for another adult

You could get an extra payment of £37.50 a week if:

  • you get Carer’s Allowance
  • you have claimed Carer's Allowance but are not being paid because you already get another benefit paying a higher amount

If you have a partner, you can both get this extra amount if you have claimed or are getting Carer's Allowance.

If you are responsible for children or young people

If you are the main carer for children or young people that live with you, you could get an additional payment.

A child is anyone under 16 years old. A young person is between 16 and 19 and must be in non-advanced education. For example, studying for GSCEs or A levels.

You will get an extra payment of £54.32 a week for each child or young person. This is increased to £64.82 a week for the first child if they were born before 6 April 2017.

If the child or young person is disabled, you will also getan extra amount of either:

  • £29.52 a week if they get DLA or PIP
  • £92.12 a week if they are blind or they get the highest rate care component of DLA, or the enhanced daily living component of PIP

You cannot get this additional payment through Pension Credit if you get Tax Credits.

If you have housing costs

You could get an extra amount to cover your housing costs, such as:

  • ground rent
  • some service charges
  • charges for tents and site rents

The amount you will get depends on your housing costs.

If you get Pension Credit, you can also apply for:

  • Council Tax Reduction
  • Housing Benefit if you rent the property you live in, or support with mortgage interest if you own it

If you have savings

If you reached State Pension age before 6 April 2016, you could also get an extra amount if you have made some provision for retirement, such as savings or a second pension.

This is called Savings Credit and is up to £13.97 a week for single people and £15.62 a week for couples.
